itokyo academy provides the official, latest versions of these forms and offers line-by-line guidance to ensure error-free completion.

itokyo academy's Expert Answers: Your Top FAQs

itokyo academy anticipates your questions. Here are in-depth answers to common queries, ensuring you have complete clarity and confidence throughout your UNITAS application journey.

Can I show Fixed Deposits (FDs) or Public Provident Fund (PPF) for proof of funds?

Generally, Japanese immigration prefers liquid funds in savings or current accounts. However, FDs nearing maturity (or that can be liquidated with proof) might be considered. PPF is usually not accepted due to its lock-in period. itokyo academy will advise on the best way to present your financial portfolio, always prioritizing what immigration authorities prefer.

My sponsor is retired. Is that acceptable?

Yes, a retired sponsor can be acceptable if they have sufficient pension income (provable via pension statements) and substantial savings that meet the >10 Lakhs INR balance requirement. The source of funds must be clearly documented (e.g., retirement benefits, past savings). itokyo academy helps structure such cases.

What if my sponsor's ITR income is slightly less than 10 Lakhs but bank balance is very high?

Both ITR income (>10 Lakhs) and bank balance (>10 Lakhs) are generally key indicators. While a very strong bank balance can be positive, a significantly lower ITR might raise questions about ongoing financial support capacity. itokyo academy will assess the overall financial profile and advise if additional co-sponsors or documentation might be needed.

Can I have multiple sponsors?

It's generally preferred to have one strong primary sponsor. Multiple sponsors (e.g., both parents) can be considered if their combined financials clearly meet the criteria and their relationship to you is easily provable. Complex multiple sponsor scenarios can weaken an application. itokyo academy advises the simplest, strongest approach.

How long does the entire application process take with itokyo academy?

From initial consultation with itokyo academy to visa approval, the process typically takes 4-6 months, depending on the intake, document readiness, and immigration processing times. itokyo academy creates a personalized timeline for you.

Do I need a medical certificate for the application?

A medical certificate is not usually required for the initial application to UNITAS or for the Certificate of Eligibility (CoE). However, some embassies might request a basic health check-up or specific tests (like TB) at the visa stamping stage. itokyo academy will inform you if this becomes necessary for your specific consulate.

What is a Certificate of Eligibility (CoE) and why is it important?

The CoE is a document issued by the Japanese immigration authorities that certifies you have met the conditions for landing in Japan. UNITAS applies for this on your behalf after they accept your application (a process managed by itokyo academy). You need the original CoE to apply for your student visa at the Japanese Embassy/Consulate in your country. It's the most crucial step before the visa itself.

Does itokyo academy or UNITAS help with accommodation?

Yes, UNITAS offers various accommodation options like dormitories or can assist with finding private apartments. itokyo academy will provide you with these options and guide you through the selection and booking process once your admission is confirmed.

Can I work part-time while studying at UNITAS? How many hours?

Yes, as a student visa holder, you can apply for a work permit that allows you to work up to 28 hours per week during school terms and up to 40 hours per week (8 hours a day) during long vacation periods. itokyo academy and UNITAS provide guidance on finding part-time jobs.

What level of Japanese should I ideally have before joining UNITAS?

While UNITAS has courses for absolute beginners, itokyo academy strongly recommends completing at least JLPT N5 level (or equivalent 150-200 study hours) before departure. This not only strengthens your visa application but also helps you adapt more quickly to life and studies in Japan.
